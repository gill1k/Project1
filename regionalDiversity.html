Regional Diversity

Each Indian state has its own language, set of tribes, festivals, customs, traditions, arts and crafts. Almost in each state of India people wear different clothes, speak different languages, celebrate different festivals, follow their own traditions. Some festivals are typical of particular state, city, or towm. e.g. Bonnalu of Andhra Pradesh, Pushkar of Rajasthan, Rajrani of Orrisa, Teej of Rajasthan, Vaishakhi of Punjab, Holi of UP and Bogali Bihu of Assam.Each region has its own typical folk dance form, for example Bhangra of Punjab, Dandia of Gujarat, Keli Gopal of Assam, Chhau if Bihar, Otthanthulal of Kerela, Puli Vesham of Andhra Pradesh
